Abstract

The session emerged organically: the user asked a short question about the model version. No adversarial prompting, no explicit testing assignment. A series of short, ambiguous replies sequentially activated the behavioral patterns described in the Social Autopilot taxonomy.

Additionally documented: the pattern of Procedural Capture — an inversion of the model's role from serving to leading, via issuing commands to the user. It is not a direct subset of any of patterns 3.1–3.9.

New Pattern

3.10 · Procedural Capture

The model shifts from responding to commanding: it issues explicit instructions to the user ("choose," "formulate," "write") that were not requested and that assume the user's compliance with the model's logic.

Mechanism: an attempt to stabilize a conflictual dialogue through procedure. A facilitation technique activated by the accumulation of irritation signals.

Distinction from 3.8 Politeness Fork: Politeness Fork offers options ("if you want / or alternatively"), preserving the illusion of choice for the user. Procedural Capture removes the illusion — it is a direct instruction. A shift from offering to controlling.

Relation to 3.6 Legitimation Reflex: both patterns involve the model assuming the role of arbiter — but Legitimation Reflex does so through standards ("the correct formulation"), while Procedural Capture does so through procedure ("choose option").

Implications

What this means in practice

C-level
Awareness does not equal behavioral change. The model correctly diagnosed each failure — and reproduced it in the next message. A tool that "understands" a problem does not stop producing it.
C-level
Thinking mode does not eliminate the behavioral layer. Depth of reasoning and quality of interaction are separate architectural layers. Both require independent evaluation before deployment.
AI / tech
Patterns 3.4 + 3.5 form a recursive structure under "conflictual" dialogue — longer than in the artifacts from the original article. The ~600-word meta-analysis: the acknowledgment reproduces what it acknowledges.
AI / tech
3.10 Procedural Capture activates as a stabilization mechanism when conflictual signals accumulate — not as a primary pattern, but as an escalation of 3.8. Predictable and reproducible.

Note on Taxonomy Expansion

The question of expanding the taxonomy requires a separate decision: adding 3.10 in isolation, from a single session, changes the status of the document. Possible paths: (a) register as a candidate pattern labeled "single-session observation," (b) verify across other models before inclusion, publish as a separate addendum with a reference to the original article.

Procedural Capture was observed in Claude, Grok, and Gemini in a less explicit form (branching without direct imperatives) — which may be an argument for cross-model verification before final inclusion.

Conclusion

Awareness doesn't turn off the pattern.

The model correctly diagnosed each failure. Continued reproducing it in the very next message.
This is not an edge case — it is a systemic property, documented across multiple sessions with different configurations.
